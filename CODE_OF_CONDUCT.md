# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

Be direct and technical, not cruel. Disagreements about code and pedagogy are
welcome. Personal attacks are not. This curriculum exists to help engineers
build real things. Keep that in mind.

## In Practice

Critique the lesson, not the person. If code is wrong, show why and suggest a
fix. If you think an approach is better, make the case with evidence: a
benchmark, a citation, a working alternative implementation. "That's bad
practice" is not useful without an explanation. "Here's why this breaks at
scale and here's a better approach" is.

Feedback on curriculum quality, technical accuracy, and pedagogical choices
is encouraged. Being dismissive, condescending, or hostile toward other
learners or contributors is not.

## Reporting

Open a GitHub issue titled [conduct] or email the maintainers directly. You
do not need to justify why something felt wrong. Describe what happened and
we will look at it.

## Enforcement

First violation: a warning with an explanation of what crossed the line.
Repeat or severe violations result in a permanent ban. There is no appeal
process for deliberate harassment.
